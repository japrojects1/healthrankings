How we test.

Our testing methodology follows a five-step process designed to eliminate bias and produce rankings you can trust with your health.

1

Category research

We consult with medical professionals to understand what matters for each health condition and product category — clinical accuracy, ease of use, data connectivity, accessibility, and more.

2

Product procurement

We purchase every product at retail from Amazon, manufacturer sites, or pharmacies. No review units, no manufacturer relationships, no special pricing.

3

Structured testing

Each product is tested against our category-specific rubric. For blood pressure monitors, that means comparing against a clinical-grade reference device. For supplements, it means third-party lab verification of contents.

4

Scoring & ranking

Products are scored across weighted categories (accuracy, usability, value, build quality, and more). The scores produce the final ranking — no manual overrides, no editorial favoritism.

5

Medical review

A licensed healthcare professional reviews the final rankings, the condition guide content, and all health claims for accuracy before publication.

Our funding model: HealthRankings earns a small commission when you purchase a product through our links. This is standard affiliate commerce — and critically, it has zero influence on our rankings. Products are ranked before affiliate links are added. We would rather lose revenue than compromise a ranking.

Questions, answered.

How does HealthRankings make money?

We earn affiliate commissions when you buy products through links on our site — typically 3–8% of the purchase price. This is the same model used by Consumer Reports, Wirecutter, and most independent review sites.

Critically: brands cannot pay for higher rankings, favorable coverage, or removal of negative findings. Our rankings are finalized before affiliate links are added.

Do manufacturers send you products to review?

No. Every product we review is purchased at retail — full price, from the same stores you shop at. We never accept review units, early access, or manufacturer-provided products. This eliminates any sense of obligation or reciprocity that could influence our assessment.

Who writes your condition guides?

Our condition guides are researched and written by our editorial team using peer-reviewed medical literature as primary sources. Every guide is then reviewed by a licensed healthcare professional on our medical review board to ensure clinical accuracy.

We cite primary sources wherever possible and clearly distinguish between established medical consensus and emerging research.

How often do you update your rankings?

We continuously monitor our product categories. Rankings are formally reviewed quarterly, but we update immediately when: a significant new product launches, a product is discontinued or recalled, a firmware update materially changes performance, or pricing shifts meaningfully.

Can a brand contact you about a review?

Brands can contact us to correct factual errors (wrong price, discontinued model, etc.) and we will verify and fix genuine mistakes. However, brands cannot request changes to scores, rankings, or editorial opinions. We do not offer paid placements, sponsored reviews, or "premium listings."

Is HealthRankings a medical resource?

HealthRankings provides health information for educational purposes. Our condition guides are medically reviewed but are not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your healthcare provider for medical decisions.

Our product reviews and rankings are designed to help you choose the best health monitoring tools — but the data from those tools should always be interpreted in consultation with your doctor.
