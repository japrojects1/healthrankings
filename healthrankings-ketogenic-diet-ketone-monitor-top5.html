Blood vs urine vs breath ketone testing: Blood ketone meters (measuring beta-hydroxybutyrate) are the gold standard — accurate throughout all stages of keto adaptation. Urine ketone strips become unreliable after 4–6 weeks as adaptation causes ketone excretion to decline. Breath acetone meters are non-invasive but less precise. For serious keto tracking, blood meters are the only reliable long-term option.

Keto-Mojo GK+
Best Overall — Dual Glucose + Ketone + Bluetooth + Lowest Strip Cost

The Keto-Mojo GK+ is the definitive ketone monitor for serious ketogenic diet practitioners. It measures both blood glucose and blood ketones on the same device — essential for diabetics on ketogenic diet monitoring both simultaneously. Ketone strips at $0.99 each (vs $4–6 for competitor strips) make frequent testing economically feasible. Bluetooth syncs all readings to the Keto-Mojo app with trend charts. A free integration with Cronometer and MyFitnessPal connects food logging to blood glucose and ketone data. Accuracy within ±10% of clinical BHB reference measurement.

Abbott Precision Xtra
Best for Diabetics on Keto — Medical-Grade Ketone + Glucose

The Abbott Precision Xtra is the only ketone/glucose meter used in clinical trials and hospital settings for both DKA monitoring and nutritional ketosis tracking — the most clinically validated dual meter available. Particularly important for Type 1 and insulin-dependent Type 2 diabetics who need the highest accuracy level for safety. The ketone test requires a separate strip type from glucose testing. Widely available at pharmacies.

Biosense Breath Ketone Analyzer
Best No-Prick Option — Breath Acetone Measurement

The Biosense is the most accurate breath ketone analyzer available — measuring exhaled acetone in ACEs (arbitrary clinical equivalents) that correlate with blood BHB. No finger pricks, no strips, no ongoing cost beyond the device. Bluetooth app tracks trends over time. Accuracy is ±15–20% vs blood measurement — adequate for tracking ketosis yes/no and general ketone level trends, but less precise than blood measurement for fine-tuning.

Dexcom G7 CGM (Glucose + Ketone Correlation)
Best for Advanced Metabolic Monitoring — Continuous Glucose Sensor

While not a ketone meter, the Dexcom G7 continuous glucose monitor is increasingly used by ketogenic diet practitioners to understand how specific foods affect blood glucose in real time — the 'glucose response mapping' approach. Seeing glucose spikes from supposedly keto-safe foods reveals individual carbohydrate tolerance. Paired with periodic ketone testing (Keto-Mojo), CGM + blood ketones provides the most complete picture of metabolic response to the ketogenic diet. Requires prescription.

Pros

  • Real-time food response mapping
  • Glucose variability data — important for metabolic health
