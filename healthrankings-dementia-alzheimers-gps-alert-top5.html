Best Health Alert & GPS Systems for Dementia & Alzheimer's

We evaluated 11 monitoring, alert, and GPS tracking systems specifically for dementia caregivers — prioritizing wandering detection (geofencing + alerts), GPS accuracy, fall detection, medication reminders, and caregiver notification reliability.

The two most critical features for dementia safety: (1) Geofencing GPS — alerts caregivers the moment a person leaves a predefined safe zone (home, care facility), not after they are already lost. (2) Ease of wearing — a device the person refuses to wear provides zero protection. Form factor compliance is as important as technical specifications.

1
AngelSense GPS Tracker
Best Overall for Dementia — Built for Cognitive Impairment

AngelSense is the only GPS tracker specifically designed and optimized for people with cognitive impairment — the device attaches to clothing with a tamper-resistant clip the wearer cannot easily remove (critical for dementia patients who try to remove devices). Real-time GPS tracking updates every 10 seconds (not every 5 minutes like many competitors), Geofencing alerts notify caregivers immediately when the person leaves home, and two-way calling allows caregivers to speak with their loved one through the device. The 'Listen-In' feature lets caregivers hear ambient audio to assess safety without alerting the person. Specifically designed for situations where the wearer may not cooperate with the safety system.

Apple Watch Series 10 (Shared Location + Fall Detection)
Best Wristwatch — Fall Detection + GPS + Medication Reminders

For early-to-moderate dementia patients who will wear and tolerate a smartwatch, Apple Watch Series 10's combination of fall detection (automatic 911 call if unresponsive), GPS sharing with family, medication reminders, and cellular connectivity makes it the most feature-rich wrist-worn safety device. Family sharing in Apple Maps shows real-time location. Requires patient cooperation and some device competency — not suitable for advanced dementia.

Tile Ultra Hidden GPS Tracker
Best Discrete Option — Hidden in Shoe or Pocket

For dementia patients who resist wearing any device on their body, the Tile Ultra can be placed discreetly in a shoe, sewn into clothing, or placed in a daily bag — allowing location tracking without patient awareness or cooperation. While it provides location rather than real-time continuous GPS (checks in when near Tile network devices), it has successfully located wandering patients. Best combined with a door alert system for early wandering detection.

Door Alert Sensor + SimpliSafe Integration
Best Home Exit Alert — Immediate Geofence at the Door

For dementia caregivers who primarily need to know the moment their loved one attempts to leave the home — often in the middle of the night — door contact sensors with loud alarms and caregiver smartphone notifications are the most cost-effective wandering prevention tool. SimpliSafe and Ring door sensors provide instant alerts. Best used as a first layer of protection before wandering reaches the point requiring GPS tracking.
