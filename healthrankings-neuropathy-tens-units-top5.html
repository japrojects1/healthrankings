Best TENS Units for Neuropathic Pain Relief

TENS for neuropathy — what the research shows: A 2010 Cochrane review found TENS reduces pain intensity in diabetic peripheral neuropathy. The optimal frequency for neuropathic pain is 2–10 Hz (low frequency) to stimulate endorphin release — different from the 80–150 Hz used for acute pain. Look for devices with specific neuropathy or nerve pain programs at low frequencies.

The iReliev ET-5050 is the only TENS device in our review with a specific neuropathy program that automatically cycles through the optimal frequency range for nerve pain (2–10 Hz) with built-in burst patterns that prevent nerve accommodation. The EMS function allows muscle stimulation that helps maintain foot muscle tone — important for preventing foot drop in advanced neuropathy. 8 programs including dedicated peripheral neuropathy, plantar fasciitis, and sciatica modes. Zynex NexWave (Prescription)
Best Clinical-Grade — Trimodal with Interferential Current

The Zynex NexWave is a prescription device that combines TENS, Interferential Current (IFC), and Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation in one unit — the same modalities used in physical therapy clinics. IFC uses two medium-frequency currents that intersect at depth to produce a low-frequency beat at the nerve — more effective for deep nerve pain than standard TENS. Requires a prescription but available for home use. Often covered by insurance for diagnosed neuropathy.
