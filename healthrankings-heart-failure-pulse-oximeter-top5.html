SpO₂ targets in heart failure: Most HF patients should maintain SpO₂ above 92–94% at rest. A drop of 3–5% from personal baseline, or any reading below 90%, requires immediate physician contact. Daily oximetry is a Class IIa recommendation in advanced HF guidelines.

The Masimo MightySat Rx uses SET signal extraction technology — identical to hospital ICU pulse oximeters. Measures SpO₂, heart rate, perfusion index, respiration rate, and pleth variability index. Customizable low-SpO₂ alarms alert patients before dangerous desaturation. Bluetooth syncs all readings to the Masimo app with trend charts for cardiologist review.

Wellue O2Ring Gen 2
Best for Overnight Monitoring — Detects Nocturnal Decompensation

HF decompensation often begins during sleep — fluid redistributes when lying flat, worsening pulmonary edema and causing nocturnal oxygen desaturations. The O2Ring worn overnight provides continuous SpO₂ monitoring with automatic desaturation event detection, silent vibration alarm, and overnight reports — exactly the data needed to detect early nighttime decompensation.
