Best Breathing Trainers for Athletic Performance

Respiratory training is the most underutilized performance lever in amateur athletics. We tested 12 breathing training devices specifically for their impact on athletic performance metrics — VO₂max, time to exhaustion, and breathing economy.

The athletic case for breathing training: Inspiratory muscle fatigue contributes to exercise-induced fatigue even in the legs — the brain reduces motor output to limb muscles when respiratory muscles are struggling. Strengthening inspiratory muscles with 6 weeks of IMT improves time to exhaustion by 15–20% and 5K performance by 45 seconds on average.

Adjustable resistance from 0–190 cmH₂O supports athletes from beginner to elite.

Best Evidence-Based — Used in VO₂max Research

The Threshold IMT appears in more peer-reviewed studies on IMT and athletic performance than any other device. Its spring-loaded threshold resistance mechanism produces consistent training loads at precisely measured pressures — the scientific gold standard for IMT research. If the published literature showing 15–20% improvement in time to exhaustion excites you, this is the device those studies used.

Relaxator Breathing Retrainer
Best for Nasal Breathing & CO₂ Tolerance

The Relaxator is unique — it specifically trains slower, lower-volume breathing to improve CO₂ tolerance and shift athletes toward nasal breathing at rest and during low-intensity exercise. CO₂ tolerance is increasingly recognized as a performance metric — higher tolerance allows the body to maintain output with less perceived breathlessness. Best used in the Patrick McKeown / Oxygen Advantage training framework.
